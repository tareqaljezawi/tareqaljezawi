OpenTango — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

OpenTango does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any personal data. The extension is designed to run entirely on your device.

What data the extension handles

When you record a guide, OpenTango captures screenshots of the browser tab you are documenting, along with basic information about the elements you click (such as a button's label and its position). This content is used only to build your guide.

Where that data goes

Everything is stored locally in your browser using IndexedDB on your own computer. Nothing is uploaded to any server, and the developer has no access to it. There are no analytics, no tracking, no advertising, and no third-party services.

On-device AI (optional)

If you use the optional "rewrite" feature, it runs Chrome's built-in on-device model (Gemini Nano) locally. Your text is not sent to any external service.

Permissions

  • activeTab — reads the URL of the tab you're on when you open the popup, so access can be requested for just that site.
  • scripting — injects the capture script into a site you're recording, and only while recording.
  • storage — saves recording state locally; your guides and images live in local IndexedDB on your device.
  • Per-site page access (requested when you click "Start recording" or "Add this site") — lets the extension screenshot that specific site. Access is granted one site at a time, never for all sites, and can be revoked anytime in your browser's extension settings.

Your control over your data

You can delete any guide from within the extension at any time, or remove all data by clearing the extension's storage in your browser. Uninstalling the extension removes its local data.
